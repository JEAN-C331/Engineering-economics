Symbol & convention glossary

Same letters are reused across factors—always tie each symbol to a period length and payment timing before substituting numbers.

Symbol Meaning Typical units Conventions on this site
P Present worth (lump sum at t = 0 or at the valuation instant). Currency (e.g., USD). Single payment unless paired with an annuity factor.
F Future worth—a one-time cash flow at the end of period n. Currency. Uses the same period index as n for i.
A Uniform amount per period (“annuity” payment). Currency per period. Ordinary annuity: payments at each end of period (default). Annuity due: payments at period beginning—shift one period or use textbook adjustment.
i Interest (or discount) rate per interest period (not always per calendar year). Dimensionless per period (enter 0.08 for 8%). Discrete compounding unless a problem states continuous r.
n Number of compounding (or payment) periods in the model. Integer periods. Must match the timing of i and of each cash flow arrow.
G Arithmetic gradient step—cash flows increase by G each period after a base. Currency per period. Requires the textbook's "0, G, 2G, …" pattern relative to tabulated (P/G) or (A/G) forms.
r, m Nominal rate per year (r) and compounding periods per year (m). r as decimal/year; m as count. Periodic rate iperiod = r/m when periods align with compounding months/quarters.
S Salvage value—estimated resale value at end of asset life. Currency. Treated as inflow at period n unless specified otherwise.
t Time index—period number in cash flow timeline. Integer period index (0, 1, 2, ..., n). t = 0 is the present; t = n is the final period.
NCF Net cash flow—receipts minus disbursements in a period. Currency per period. Positive = inflow (receipt); Negative = outflow (disbursement).
NPW / NPV Net Present Worth / Net Present Value. Currency. Sum of discounted cash flows at MARR. Accept if NPW ≥ 0.
AW Annual Worth—equivalent uniform annual amount. Currency per period. AW = EUAB - EUAC. Accept if AW ≥ 0.
EUAC Equivalent Uniform Annual Cost. Currency per period. Converts all costs to equivalent uniform annual amount.
EUAB Equivalent Uniform Annual Benefit. Currency per period. Converts all benefits to equivalent uniform annual amount.
CR Capital Recovery—annual cost to recover initial investment. Currency per period. CR = P(A/P,i,n) - S(A/F,i,n).
MARR Minimum Attractive Rate of Return. Dimensionless per period (decimal). Hurdle rate for accept/reject decisions. Used as i in PW/AW analysis.
IRR Internal Rate of Return. Dimensionless per period (decimal or percent). Interest rate where NPW = 0. Accept if IRR ≥ MARR.
ΔIRR Incremental Internal Rate of Return. Dimensionless per period (decimal or percent). IRR on incremental cash flow between alternatives. Accept increment if ΔIRR ≥ MARR.

How to solve problems & common pitfalls

A compact workflow plus mistakes that cost points on exams.

  1. Draw the cash-flow diagram. Mark the period length (week/month/year), then place every known inflow/outflow.
  2. Align i and n. Convert a nominal annual r with compounding m to the periodic rate that matches your payment spacing.
  3. Pick factors. Map each arrow to (P/F), (F/P), (P/A), (F/A), etc., or combine patterns (base + gradient).
  4. Compare alternatives fairly. Same study horizon, MARR, and tax/treatment assumptions unless the prompt says otherwise.

Ordinary vs annuity due

Tabulated (P/A) and (F/A) factors assume the first A occurs at the end of period 1. If the first cash flow is at t = 0, shift with one (F/P) step or use the “annuity due” adjustment from your text—do not plug values blindly.

MARR vs IRR

MARR is the stated hurdle for PW analysis. IRR is the rate that zeros NPW for a given cash-flow pattern. For mutually exclusive projects, ranking by IRR can disagree with NPW—use NPW (or incremental analysis) at the same MARR unless instructed otherwise.

Nominal vs effective

Compare financing or savings offers using effective rates when compounding frequencies differ. A lower quoted nominal rate with daily compounding can beat a higher nominal rate with annual compounding.
